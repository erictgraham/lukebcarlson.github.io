ARCH

Augmented Reality for Cultural Heritage

Working on a group of 4, we research, designed, and prototyped an AR application for an exhibit in the Harry Randsom Center.

Overview

For our Rapid Prototyping class, we were tasked with creating an experience that would engage an targeted audience for an Arts & Crafts exhibition at the Harry Randosom Center. Working alongside the School of Architecutre, we seamlessly integrated new technology into the exhibt.

Research

The HRC is in a location where a sizable proportion of the surrounding population shares similar personality characteristics to “the Weekender”. We decided to emphasize this persona because of the representation in the local area, and their propensity towards technology and likelihood to use an app.

Our Processs

To help guide us in our design, we created a journey map for the "Weekender" persona we developed. We emphasised the pre and post expeereince just as heavily as the expereince itself. By thouroughly considering each step in the exhibit, we were able to deliver a product that effectively met our users needs.

Our Designs

Our app is not just for this specific event, it could be used for all HRC events in the future. We try to help people figure out how to start their journey in the exhibition without any difficulties. We thought that using augmented reality would be suitable for this project. We integrated a “My Collection” to archives objects our users have seen or haven’t seen to give them some ideas of where to go. We really value the post-experiences, so in our user profile, we set up features for our users to recall their experiences even after they go back home.

Deliverables
